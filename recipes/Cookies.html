Chocolate Chip Cookies (2 dozen)

Description

Chocolate chip cookies are a beloved and classic treat enjoyed by people of all ages. These delectable cookies are characterized by their soft and chewy texture, golden brown exterior, and gooey pockets of melted chocolate. They are often made with a combination of flour, butter, sugar, eggs, vanilla extract, baking soda, and of course, chocolate chips.

The aroma of freshly baked chocolate chip cookies wafting through the air is enough to make anyone's mouth water. As they bake in the oven, the sweet scent of caramelizing sugars and melting chocolate fills the kitchen, creating an irresistible temptation. The anticipation builds as the cookies gradually turn golden, with the edges slightly crisp while the centers remain wonderfully soft and tender.

Ingredients

  • 1 and 1/2 cups packed dark brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup (1 stick) unsalted butter, melted and cooled
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 3/4 teaspoon baking soda
  • One 12-ounce bag semisweet chocolate chips

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 375 degrees F. Line two baking sheets with parchment paper or silicone sheets.
  2. In a large bowl, using an electric hand mixer, mix the sugar, butter, vanilla and eggs until light and fluffy. In another bowl, whisk the flour, salt and baking soda. Stir the dry ingredients into the wet ingredients, then gently fold in the chocolate chips.
  3. Scoop heaping tablespoons of the dough onto the prepared pans, spacing the cookies about 2-inches apart. Bake until golden brown, about 15 minutes. Using a spatula, transfer the hot cookies to a cooling rack.
  4. Store the cookies in a tightly sealed resealable plastic bag for up to 3 days.
